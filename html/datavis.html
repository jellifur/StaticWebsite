Visualizing U.S. Education Data

(Data Visualization at University of Washington)
Team Members: Meredith Anderson, Jenn Chan, Sophie Kahan, Vera Ranguelova

insert_chart INITIAL INVESTIGATION

BACKGROUND

At the inception of the project, we reflected upon our own difficulties in choosing post-secondary education. We had to consider available financing, institutional reputation, distance to home, extracurriculars, choice of programs and majors, etc. We wondered whether data, rather than just our parents and guidance counselors, could have made this process easier.

INITIAL DESIGN QUESTION

How might we help high school and undergraduate college students make decisions for their post-secondary education?

TARGET USERS

High school seniors in the U.S. education system

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

  • The College Board
  • Chegg
  • Khan Academy
  • University websites (e.g. admissions.rpi.edu)
In summary, these companies and nonprofit organizations offer students articles, quizzes and tools to find, compare, and apply to schools, and pay for tuition. While the College Board and Khan Academy offer students advice on how to succeed, they do so with articles rather than interactive tools or visualizations for students to make their own decisions. In response to these other tools, the first iteration of our project aimed to fill a gap by providing students the data to make informed decisions through an interactive visualization.

DATA SET

We found data sets from the National Center for Education Statistics (NCES), which is a federally funded organization that collects and analyzes data related to education in the United States. We initially decided to use the NCES Study of 1988, a longitudinal study of about 17,000 eighth grade students over the course of more than a decade.

INTERVIEWS & PERSONAS

We interviewed 7 students and developed 2 personas based on these individuals.
  • Dustin - Has a difficulty choosing a major based on his interest in journalism or a major in engineering based on job security?
  • Rebecca - Wants to find an academic path that would best meet her interest in genetics, philanthropy, and making a positive impact on humanity?

SKETCHES & USER FEEDBACK

Feedback on the sketches, and further probing revealed that students' main concern regarding postgraduate decision making was whether or not they would get a job. There was also a lack of interest in data visualizations. Therefore, we pivoted our design question and target users.
